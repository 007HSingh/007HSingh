### How it started:

As a kid I was always fascinated by how applications worked: Tux Paint, Subway Surfers (I had no idea it was running on an emulator).
Always knew I'd take CS in college.

Tried to dual boot. Corrupted my boot partition. Took it as a sign, installed Ubuntu, and never looked back.
Did some distro hopping, eventually landed on NixOS for its declarative nature and haven't left since.
Went down the ricing rabbit hole somewhere in there and picked up Lua along the way.
Learned Python building small interactive programs. Nothing serious, just enough to get hooked.

Then came CS50. That's what got me into CS fundamentals and the kind of low-level control C gives you.
Built a simple custom shell for the final project. Something clicked after that.

### What I'm doing now:

CS student at IIIT Kalyani. Currently working towards building full-stack Android apps, Jetpack Compose on the frontend and Spring Boot on the back.

Also trying to sharpen the mathematical side of things to go deeper into the theoretical parts of CS.
Getting more serious about Nix and looking to make some actual contributions to the ecosystem.

Outside of that: chess on Chess.com, and whatever book caught my eye, either something whose cover looked interesting or one of the usual famous ones.
